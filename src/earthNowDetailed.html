Resource Depletion

Not recycling means that the environment must be continuously mined for raw materials to meet production demand. As a result, limited natural resources that are essential to many industries, such as minerals, fossil fuels, and forests, are being depleted. This may eventually result in a shortage, greater expenses, and more environmental harm from extraction operations.

Environmental Pollution

Environmental contamination results from things that could be recycled, but ending up in landiflls or incinerators. Methane for example, a powerful greenhouse gas, is released during the decomposition of organic waste, and soil and groundwater are contaminated by the leaching of hazardous chemicals from electronics and other non-recycled materials. This pollution puts wildlife in danger, damages ecosystems, and endangers people's health.

Waste Management Challenges

Waste management systems are put under more stress when recycling isn't done. This pollution puts wildlife in danger, damages ecosystems, and endangers people's health.
